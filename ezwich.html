Project Overview

E-zwich is a biometric smart card payment system. Why E-zwich?

The e-zwich payment system provides payments services from the government to national service personnel, pensioners, NABCO trainers, teachers and nurses trainers and some other groups of employees. Some of its users privately and publicly expressed negative opinions about the e-zwich services. These views suggest that, their services are frustrating to use and that it imposes a poor experience on customers. Some have claimed this as the main reason why they stopped using their cards immediately after their national service or withdraw their money and redeposit it into another bank after they receive payment on the cards from their various employers.

Product Vision

For our e-zwich card holders who need to be able to make transactions on the go, the E-zwich app is a mobile device application that will enable valid card holders to interact, transfer money, pay bills, save time and build a closer relationship with us. Unlike the existing method, our mobile app will enable the users to receive up to date notifications on their account and system.

My Process

For the team to better understand our target users and make sure we are solving the right problem and pain points for them, we adapted the design thinking process. We tried to make sure the user is always at the center of the product we are designing for them. Therefore, we make sure they are 100% involve in the design process from empathizing with users through conducting user research, surveys, build user personas, user journey, to defining the need of the user, ideating possible solutions using the various techniques such as; competitive analysis, crazy 8 etc, to prototyping the ideas generated and conducting usability studies to test our early prototypes with our target users for the team to be able to develop a highly usable and accessible product wit the user always in mind.

Empathize

The first step we took towards solving the problem we identified was to try and understand the people we are designing for. The goal here is to focus on understanding user behaviours, needs, and motivations through observation and feedbacks by priritizing the user of our product. Conducting reserach helped us brige the gap between what we as a business thinks the user needs and what actually the user need by building user personas, crafting user stories, and building user journey maps to help us understand and humanize our target users.

Empathy Mapping

To further build more understanding with our target users, we developed an empathy map to helps us explain and visualize everything we learned about our target users. Based on the research conducted, we developed an aggregated empathy map for our users where we consolidate feedback from a group of users to enable us identify their pain points and visualize them as a whole. An empathy map consists of four squares, which show what the user says, does, thinks, and feels.

Persona

After feeling empathy for users and building empathy maps to identify users pain points, we started to personify our target users. Personas are fictional users whose goals and characteristics represents the needs of a larger group of users. We created persona that represent each group of users with similar characteristics that we have learned about from through our research. Personas are key to the design process because they reflect the lifestyle of users and give us an idea of how to meet users needs and the challenges we are trying to solve for them.

User stories

User stories are one sentence story told from the persona‚Äôs point of view to inspire and inform design decisions. We wrote user stories to help us deepen our understanding of our user groups for our product. We chose user stories to guide us prioritize design goals, unite the team around a common goal, inspire empathetic design decisions by making our approach user centered.

A journey map is an illustrations of what our target group goes through to achieve their goals when using a product. Since this is a new product, our team looked the current journey the user takes to reach their goals in other for us to identify iprovement opportunities within their current flow by considering the emotions attached to each step they take to reach their goal.

Ideate Pharse

In the ideation pharse, the team came up with lots of ideas that we assumed are the best possible solutions to the users problems we have identified. We later screened the ideas based on if the ideas are feasible, desirable, and viable. In other to have lots of ideas, we also used some other ideation methods such as competitive analysis, How Might We, crazy 8 among others

Competitve audit

We know that it's important to come up with lots of ideas before deciding on one solution. A competitive audit is just one of the tools to explore ideas for our designs, so we can learn from others or our competitors about what has worked and not worked for them. We start by identifying some of our key direct and indirect competitors in the space that we are targeting, reviewed their product offers, and examined what they do well and what they could do better and also consider how they talk about themselves. Using this tool helped the team identified the gaps in the market and how we can address these gaps through our app.

How Might We

After conducting our competitive analysis, the team believed we have to generate more ideas so we decided to use another ideation technique called ‚ÄúHow Might We?‚Äù. Here, the team used the how might to translate the problems we identified into opportunities for design. The word "how" encourages us to explore a bunch of ideas instead of moving forward with only one idea for the solution. The word "how" suggests that we don't have an answer yet. The word "might" emphasizes that our ideas are possible solutions, not the only solution. Finally, the word "we" suggests a collaborative effort. Coming up with ideas requires teamwork. So with our problem statement, one of our how might we statement reads; How Might We design a solution for Sika to get access to the fund in her E-zwich account easy and fast?

Crazy 8

The Crazy Eights exercise is a great way to get ideas flowing for any design problem you need to solve. With Crazy Eights, we sketch eight different designs, each with a new idea for solving the user‚Äôs problem. The best part? It only takes eight minutes! Using the crazy 8 ideation technique is a bit of the fun part with our ideation process because it involve a bit of sketching. With this technique, the team sketched solutions that each member assumed could be a way our problems we identified can be resolve. Crazy Eights lets us compare ideas, see everyone's different ideas and narrow down the list of ideas before moving on with the best solutions. This is a bit ‚Äúcrazy‚Äù because each member in the team has only one minute to spend on each idea and we have to generate eight ideas each within eight minutes.

Userflows

Based on the information learned about our potential users, through research to help identify the needs a user will have when interacting with the product, we created a user flow. A user flow is a path taken by a typical user on an app or website so they can complete a task from start to finish. Our here goal is to map how users achieve a specific goal as they move through a product. That way, we have a clear overview of the product's functionality before the design process begins.

Wireframes

A wireframe is a basic outline of a digital experience, like an app or a website. As the name suggests, wireframes look like they were created with wires. They're mostly lines and shapes with some text. So why do UX designers create wireframes? Wireframes establish the basic structure of a page. before any visual considerations, like color or images, are added. Wireframes serve as an outline to get the team on the same page early in the project. How do I organize information on the page in a way that makes sense for users and also highlight the intended function of the product

Usability Testing

Since making changes to a high fidelity mockups can be a very difficult task, we decided to conducted our usability test with our target users with our low fidelity prototype to see how they interact with our solution to the problem that we are trying to solve. By doing this exercise we were able to determine how easy it is for the user to complete a core task in our product.

During the study we observed the participants interact with the app to see how they complete the task. Then we used the insights from our study to make improvement to our designs. This study was a moderated usability study and we asked questions to get more understanding of how they interact with the app by completing tasks assigned to them.

After making these changes to improve the app based on the insights from the study, the team decided to conduct one last study with the user but this time with the high fidelity prototype. This helped us identified some usabilty and accessibility issues with the choice of color and typography.

Onboarding Screens

Users after lunching the app are presented with onboarding screens. These screens are to serve as a guide that introduces some of the key tasks that the user can perform on our application and also gives the user the impression of what the mobile application is all about.

Login and Sign up screens

Login and signup screens gives the user the chance to either register or log into their account. New users will have to fill out the registration form to complete their registration process before they can have access to their dashboard. Whiles existing users can also use the login screen to login to interact with the app.

User dashboard

The first screen that is displayed to the user after successfully registering is the empty state of ‚ÄúNo card found‚Äù. This is to alert the user that they need to add their e-zwich cards to their account in other to make transactions. After the card is added then they get to see the apps main dashboard where they can find their main account balance, user profile, tarnscation history and perform main tasks such as transfering money, making bill payment and buying airtime.

Transfer fund screen

One of the main task that users want to perform on the app is to be able to transfer funds from their e-zwich account to other electronic money service providers. The transfer fund screens displays a variety of destinations for the user to choose from in other to send the money to. The user is asked to fill out the recipient details basically their phone number if it is a mobile money transfer, then they enter the amount that they want to send. The most important part of this is where they get to verify their fingerprints to confirm payment. Payment is only made when the fingerprint verification is successfully completed.

User account management

User account management screens give the users the control over their account. From this screens users can make changes to their account like deleting a card they had added but no longer in use or view notifications, contact customer support, privacy and security, and also read about the application. They can also logout from their account from their account as well from the user account management screens.
